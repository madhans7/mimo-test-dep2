Green Printing: Eco-Friendly Tips to Reduce Paper Waste on Campus

M
MIMO Printing Team
Published on July 7, 2026 • 6 min read

While the world is steadily moving toward digital documentation, there are times when physical paper prints are absolutely necessary for academic and professional work. Lab reports, thesis submissions, reading guides, official application forms, and identity documents still require physical sheets. However, the cumulative environmental impact of printing across thousands of students — carbon emissions, energy consumption, deforestation, and paper waste — can be enormous if not managed thoughtfully.

At MIMO, we believe that convenience should not come at the cost of the environment. By adopting a few smart printing habits, every student can dramatically reduce their ecological footprint, save significant money over a full academic year, and still get perfect physical prints for every assignment and submission. In this comprehensive guide, we will cover every practical eco-friendly printing strategy available to campus students today.

1. The Power of Double-Sided (Duplex) Printing

Duplex printing is by far the single most effective way to reduce paper waste in your academic printing habits. When you enable double-sided printing, the printer automatically outputs content on both sides of each sheet — halving the total number of physical pages needed for any document.

  • Paper Savings: Duplex printing cuts your paper consumption by exactly 50%. A 30-page research report becomes just 15 physical sheets. A 100-page thesis drops to 50 sheets. Over a full academic year, a single student can save hundreds of sheets of paper just from this one setting change.
  • Cost Savings: Since MIMO charges per printed side, duplex printing on 15 sheets costs the same as 30 single-sided pages — but you save the physical paper. Combined with page range selection, your per-document costs drop significantly.
  • Weight Reduction: Duplex documents are literally half the weight of simplex equivalents. Carrying a double-sided 200-page thesis in your bag is much more comfortable than the single-sided version.
  • Professional Appearance: Double-sided documents look more professional and compact when submitted in person. Most faculty and examiners prefer double-sided submissions for lengthy reports.
  • MIMO Compatibility: Our Brother monochrome laser printers support full automatic hardware duplexing. Simply toggle the "Double-Sided" option in the MIMO print settings panel before confirming your order.

2. Use Multi-Page Layout (N-Up Printing)

If you are printing PowerPoint slides for study revision, lecture notes, or reference reading material, you almost certainly do not need one full A4 page per slide. Most slides can be read comfortably when printed 2 or 4 per page.

The math is compelling: if you configure your PDF to print 4 slides per page and enable duplex, a 40-slide lecture presentation fits onto just 5 sheets of paper. Compare that to 40 sheets for simplex, single-slide printing — an 87.5% reduction in paper use.

  • 2 Pages Per Sheet: Best for documents with moderate text density — notes with explanatory paragraphs, assignment instructions, or summary sheets. Text remains easily readable without a magnifying glass.
  • 4 Pages Per Sheet: Best for image-heavy slides, diagram reference sheets, or minimal-text slide decks. Text may be small but still readable for most students with normal vision.
  • Combined with Duplex: 4-up duplex (4 pages per side, double-sided) means 8 slide pages per physical sheet — a 87.5% paper saving versus single-sided, one-per-page printing.
  • How to Set Up in PDF: In PowerPoint, go to File → Print → Handouts → 4 Slides Per Page → Save as PDF. In Adobe Acrobat, choose File → Print → Page Sizing & Handling → Multiple → Pages per sheet.

3. Always Check Your Print Preview Before Paying

One of the most environmentally wasteful printing behaviors is the "reprint cycle" — printing a document, noticing a formatting problem, correcting it, and printing the entire thing again. On a campus with thousands of students, this cycle generates a staggering amount of unnecessary paper waste and cost.

MIMO's dashboard shows you a live page count and layout confirmation before you confirm payment. Use this to your advantage:

  • Check for trailing blank pages caused by empty paragraphs at the end of your Word document. A 10-page report accidentally becoming 11 pages (last page blank) costs an extra print and wastes one sheet entirely.
  • Verify page orientation is correct (portrait vs. landscape) for each section of mixed-orientation documents.
  • Confirm the page count matches your expectation. An unexpected high count may mean you accidentally included an extra blank section or attached an unintended appendix.
  • Before uploading, open your PDF and zoom in to 150% on a few pages to check image sharpness and text clarity. Blurry images discovered after printing require a costly reprint.
  • Check that your headers, footers, and page numbers are fully visible within the printable area and not getting clipped.

4. Print Only the Pages You Actually Need

If you are printing a chapter from a research paper, a specific section from a textbook, or selected pages from a large PDF reference guide, you rarely need the index, bibliography, table of contents, cover page, or blank end pages.

MIMO's print settings panel allows you to specify a custom page range for every print job. Instead of printing all 80 pages of a research paper, you can print pages 12 to 34 (the specific chapter you need), saving 58 pages of paper, toner, and money in a single job.

  • Single range: Enter "12-34" to print only that chapter.
  • Multiple ranges: Enter "5, 12-20, 45, 60-65" to print specific pages and ranges in one job.
  • Reverse order: Enter pages in reverse order if your document needs to be collated in a specific sequence.
  • Skip cover pages: Academic papers often have a full-page cover with just the title and author. Skip page 1 if you don't need to print the cover for a personal study copy.

5. Choose Black & White When Colour Isn't Essential

Colour inkjet cartridges require complex chemical pigments — cyan, magenta, yellow, and black — each with a significantly heavier environmental manufacturing lifecycle compared to standard carbon-based black toner powder used in laser printers. The extraction, synthesis, packaging, and shipping of colour ink cartridges generates measurably more carbon emissions per page compared to black laser printing.

Unless you are printing a design project, colour-critical data visualization, a medical image, or a presentation where colour is genuinely required for comprehension — always default to black & white laser printing. It is:

  • Much cheaper: ₹2.30 per page (B&W laser) versus ₹10 per page (colour inkjet) — more than 4x the cost difference.
  • Faster spooling: Monochrome laser prints spool 3-5x faster than colour inkjet, reducing energy consumption per job.
  • Lower environmental impact: Carbon toner is a simpler compound with a shorter manufacturing chain than multi-component colour ink sets.
  • Better readability for text: Black laser toner on white paper produces the sharpest, highest-contrast text output — better than colour inkjet for reading and studying.

6. The Environmental Cost of Printing at Scale

To understand why these habits matter at a campus scale, consider the following rough estimates for a university with 10,000 students:

  • If each student prints an average of 200 pages per academic year: 2,000,000 pages total per year.
  • One ream of A4 paper (500 sheets) weighs approximately 2.5 kg and represents roughly 5% of a single tree.
  • 2,000,000 pages = 4,000 reams = approximately 10,000 kg of paper per year from one campus.
  • If 30% of those pages are unnecessary reprints due to formatting errors (a conservative industry estimate): that's 600,000 wasted sheets per year — equivalent to roughly 120 full trees.
  • Adopting duplex printing university-wide cuts that 2,000,000 pages to 1,000,000 physical sheets — a 50% paper reduction saving approximately 60 trees per year per campus.

These numbers make it clear that individual printing habits, multiplied across a large campus, have a genuine and significant environmental impact. Small choices made by each student collectively add up to thousands of trees over a decade.

7. Go Digital When Possible: When NOT to Print

The greenest print is the one you don't make. Before hitting the print button, honestly evaluate whether a physical copy is truly necessary:

  • Study notes: Annotate PDFs directly on your tablet or phone using apps like Notability, GoodNotes, or Adobe Acrobat. This replaces a huge category of personal study prints.
  • Draft reviews: Share documents with study group members digitally via Google Docs for collaborative editing and feedback. Only print the final submission version.
  • Reference material: Keep PDF versions of textbooks, research papers, and reading guides on your device. Use split-screen on a tablet to read alongside your work document.
  • Internal submissions: Many professors now accept digital PDF submissions via LMS platforms. Check if your assignment can be submitted digitally before printing.
  • Proof copies: Instead of printing a proof copy to check formatting, use your PDF viewer's print preview at 100% zoom, or use an online print preview simulator.
